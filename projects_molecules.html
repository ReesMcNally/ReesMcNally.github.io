Ultracold Molecules

For my PhD work, I investigate and build systems for the direct laser cooling of diatomic molecules. Ultracold studies of atomic systems have enabled a huge range of exciting work, building ultra-precise sensors, exploring new phases of matter, and (recently) offering insights into complex condensed matter systems. While atoms are not simple, and experiments with them are not easy, even diatomic molecules are immensely more complex. This makes experiments are harder, but the potential applications are richer. I hope my work with molecules will help pave the way for these new applications. Additional information can be found on my groups website, and in the publications/pictures section.
