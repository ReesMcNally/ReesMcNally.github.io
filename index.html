Introduction/Background

Hi! My name is Rees McNally and I am a scientist/tech enthusiast/math geek living in NYC. I am currently working towards my PhD in Proffessor Tanya Zelevinsky's lab, working on (and fighting against) ultracold molecules. We hope to use ultracold molecules to test a wide range of fundemental physics, asking LHC sized questions with table sized experiments. This work is extremely rewarding, and I really enjoy the range of skills I need/learn every day in the lab.

Before I Joined Tanya's group, I worked on atomic clocks under the direction of Proffesor Jun Ye at JILA, in the beautiful town of Boulder Colorado. While in Jun's group, I had the privledge of working on the most accurate clock ever made (as of 4/18/2017 anyways) and it was a really great introduction to precision measurements. Though I live in NYC right now, Boulder will always be a special place for me.

Pior to settling on atomic/molecular physics, I was lucky enough to work on a large number of projects. These included meaningful contributions to two satellites (one which Orbital Science Corporation Dropped into the Ocean, and one SpaceX launched to the heavens), as well as brief stint as an applied mathematician working on compressed sensing algorithms. I graduated from CU Boulder with degrees in engineering physics, applied mathematics, and a minor in electrical engineering.

All of these exciting oppurtunities have made me really appreciate how each discpline has a unique skill set, and I actively try to step out of my own field. There are too many exciting problems out there to focus too much time on just a subset. If anyone has any questions about my past research, fun project ideas, Columbia, the University of Colorado at Boulder, JILA, or anything else feel free to contact me (reeslmcnally@gmail.com).
